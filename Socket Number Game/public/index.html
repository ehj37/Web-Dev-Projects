The Number Game

You open your eyes. You have no clue where you are, and you feel a dull, throbbing pain in your head. As you come to your senses, you sit up. You're greeted by the sight of a camp fire, and basked in its warm glow you can make out a number of silhouetted figures along its perimeter. You recognize none of them. You call out, but nobody seems to hear. Frustrated, you attempt to get up, only to find that you can't. Suddenly, amidst the fire's crackles and hisses, you hear a voice. "One," it says loudly. Another chimes in. "Two". "Three," you say before you can stop yourself. "Four" says another. "Five". Suddenly, the word "six" rings out from two members of the circle. You scowl at them. Once again, silence befalls the group. You can feel the rage building up inside of you. To think, you could have made it to seven if not for that careless blunder. Cutting through the silence like a blade, a voice sings through the night: "one". "Two," you say. "Three". "Four". And so on...

"The number game," as I call it, it a dumb game to be played when there's no conversation to be had. The group's goal is to count up from zero. Anybody can start by saying "one", and then a different member of the group chimes in with "two". No member of the group can say two numbers in a row (i.e. someone can't rattle off "onetwothreefourfivesix" to advance the counter), and no coordination can be made relating to who says what when. This counting up continues until someone says a number that breaks the sequence, at which point the counter resets to zero. This game is best played with a large group, as it becomes more and more difficult to gauge when there's an opening to advance the counter without coming into conflict with someone else's attempt.
